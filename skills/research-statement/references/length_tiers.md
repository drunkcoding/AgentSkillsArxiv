# Length Tiers

The length cap is the single biggest determinant of content allocation. Following the brief is non-negotiable — over-length is one of the most-cited automatic-rejection reasons at European universities, and disciplined under-length signals editing skill.

## Length Tiers and Their Markets

| Tier | Length | Typical Markets | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1: 1 page** | ~500–600 words | UK Russell Group, some EU short-form | Single-spaced, 11pt, 1" margins |
| **Tier 2: 2 pages** | ~1000–1200 words | Dutch, many EU, some UK | Single-spaced, 11pt |
| **Tier 3: 3 pages** | ~1500–1800 words | Mixed EU / Dutch ceiling, some Canadian | Single-spaced, 11pt |
| **Tier 4: 4–5 pages** | ~2000–2500 words | US R1 STEM / medical / lab-heavy fields when field norms support it | Single-spaced, 11pt or 12pt |
| **Tier 5: 7–10 pages** | ~3500–5000 words | Some humanities, some social sciences | Often double-spaced |

If the brief specifies a range (e.g., "1–3 pages"), **target the middle**: 2 pages. Going to the cap signals padding; going below the floor signals laziness.

## Allocation Tables

### Tier 1: 1 Page (~500–600 words)

| Section | Share | Words |
|---|---|---|
| Opening / vision | 20% | ~120 |
| Past work (1–2 themes, very compressed) | 30% | ~180 |
| Future research (1 line, compressed) | 35% | ~200 |
| Fit + closing | 15% | ~80 |

**What gets cut:** second future-research line, all but 1 named faculty in fit, the "high-risk" ambitious line, most quantification details. The 1-page statement is essentially a research vision pitch.

### Tier 2: 2 Pages (~1000–1200 words)

| Section | Share | Words |
|---|---|---|
| Opening / vision | 12% | ~150 |
| Past work (2–3 themes) | 32% | ~380 |
| Future research (2 lines) | 38% | ~440 |
| Fit | 12% | ~140 |
| Closing / credo | 6% | ~70 |

**What gets cut:** the third future line (or compressed to a "and beyond" paragraph), heavy methodology details in past work, the second framing paragraph in opening.

### Tier 3: 3 Pages (~1500–1800 words)

| Section | Share | Words |
|---|---|---|
| Opening / vision | 10% | ~170 |
| Past work (2–3 themes) | 35% | ~580 |
| Future research (2–3 lines) | 40% | ~660 |
| Fit | 10% | ~170 |
| Closing | 5% | ~80 |

**Most flexible tier.** All sections can carry full weight. Add a high-risk third future line if it strengthens the agenda.

### Tier 4: 4–5 Pages (~2000–2500 words)

| Section | Share | Words |
|---|---|---|
| Opening / vision (often 2 paragraphs) | 12% | ~250 |
| Past work (3 themes) | 32% | ~700 |
| Future research (3 lines) | 42% | ~950 |
| Fit | 10% | ~220 |
| Closing | 4% | ~100 |

**Risk at this tier: padding.** US reviewers complain about 4-page statements that should have been 2. Cut ruthlessly. Every paragraph must justify its length.

**US caveat:** official US university guidance conflicts by field. CMU / Cornell / Penn / Yale / UConn / Notre Dame often recommend 1–4 pages; MIT / Harvard / Caltech STEM guidance often tolerates 3–5; Berkeley Math can be longer but expects an accessible executive summary. Use Tier 4 only when the job ad, discipline, or mentor examples justify it.

### Tier 5: 7–10 Pages (humanities)

Different conventions apply. The humanities research statement frequently centers on a **book project** (the dissertation-to-book trajectory) and a second-book sketch. See `discipline_variants.md` for full guidance. Approximate allocation:

| Section | Share |
|---|---|
| Intellectual framing | ~10% |
| Dissertation-to-book project | ~40% |
| Second book / next research | ~30% |
| Articles / chapters / public scholarship | ~10% |
| Methodological / theoretical contribution | ~10% |

## What Gets Cut at Each Step Down

The hierarchy of cuts when reducing length:

1. **First to go:** the optional high-risk future line.
2. **Second:** methodology details in past work that belong in cited papers.
3. **Third:** secondary themes that don't strongly serve the vision.
4. **Fourth:** cross-cutting-initiative mention in fit paragraph.
5. **Fifth:** the second framing paragraph in opening.
6. **Sixth:** the closing credo (collapsed into one sentence in fit paragraph).
7. **Last:** the vision sentence — never cut.

## Format Defaults

When format is unspecified:

- **Font:** 11pt Times / Computer Modern / a serif font. 12pt is acceptable but uses more space. Sans-serif fonts read as informal in academic settings.
- **Spacing:** Single-spaced unless brief specifies double.
- **Margins:** 1 inch / 2.5 cm all sides. Do not narrow margins to fit more text — reads as desperate.
- **Headings:** Optional for 3+ page statements; rare for 1–2 page. If used, keep 3–5 headings maximum and use bold rather than larger fonts.
- **References / citations:** Footnotes for own papers if cited. Avoid bibliography unless the brief asks for one — it eats space.

## Detection Heuristic

If unsure of the target length, look at:

1. The brief's explicit instruction (always wins).
2. The institution's prior advertisements / recent searches.
3. The region default: UK = 1, Dutch = 2, mixed-EU = 2–3, US broad = 2–3, US R1 STEM = 3–4.
4. The cover-letter expected length: a 1-page cover letter often pairs with a 2-page research statement; a longer cover letter often pairs with a 3-page statement.

When in doubt, **2 pages is the safe default**. It works for most non-US searches and even many US ones.

For US searches specifically, when no limit is given: use **3 pages** for research-intensive STEM / engineering / life-science R1 jobs, **2 pages** for broad teaching-research or primarily undergraduate institutions, and **discipline-specific norms** for math, humanities, and lab-heavy biomedical fields.
