# Terminology Map

Different briefs use different names for what is essentially the same genre — but the names are **not interchangeable**. Each carries specific obligations.

## The Document Name → Obligation Map

| Brief asks for... | What it actually means | Distinctive obligations |
|---|---|---|
| **Research Statement** | Standard research statement | Default genre; covers past + future + (light) fit. |
| **Statement of Research** | Synonym; same as above | Same as above. |
| **Research Vision** | Future-heavier statement, more strategic | **Vision sentence emphasized**; future:past ratio shifts to 60:40 or 70:30; often used in Dutch positions ("onderzoeksvisie"). |
| **Research Plan** | Future-heavier; specific projects | **Specific projects required** with timelines, methods, expected outputs; often used in EU and Asian positions. |
| **Future Research Statement** | Future-only or future-heavy | Past work compressed to ≤30%; future-research section expanded. Sometimes paired with a separate "Past Research Statement". |
| **5-Year Research Plan** | Specifically future, specifically dated | Year-by-year plan. Identify outputs per year. Sometimes paired with budget. |
| **Research Pitch** | Compressed (often 1 page) vision document | Pure vision + 1 future line; equivalent to Tier 1 length. |
| **Research Proposal** | Often a specific funding-shaped proposal | More technical detail; preliminary results; methods section; explicit budget vector. May be 5–10 pages. **Different genre.** Treat closer to a grant proposal. |
| **Research Statement and Plan** | Combined research + future | Standard structure; the "and Plan" wording slightly raises future-section expectations. |
| **Statement of Research Interests** | Often light / early-career | Lighter on past evidence; emphasizes interests + direction. Sometimes used for postdoc applications. |
| **Research Highlights** | List of past achievements | Past-only; often a 1-page bulleted list of papers and impacts. Pairs with a separate "Research Plan". |
| **Research Agenda** | Future research agenda | Future-only; similar to Research Plan but less project-specific. |

## The Disambiguation Workflow

When you encounter a brief, ask:

1. **Which exact phrase does the brief use?** ("Research Statement"? "Research Vision"? "5-Year Plan"?)
2. **Are there OTHER documents requested?** (CV, cover letter, teaching statement, diversity statement, references list, research highlights.)
3. **What does each OTHER document NOT cover, that this one must?**

Example: if the brief asks for a "Research Vision" + "Research Highlights" + "Teaching Statement" + "CV", then:
- Research Highlights covers past work in detail.
- Research Vision should therefore go heavier on **future** + **animating question** + **strategic fit** — past work compressed to evidence pointers.
- Teaching is offloaded to the teaching statement.
- This is a Dutch / EU pattern.

Example: if the brief asks for a "Research Statement" + "Cover Letter" + "CV", then:
- Cover letter carries personalization and fit (light).
- Research Statement carries everything else: past + future + (medium) fit.
- This is a US pattern.

## The Most Common Confusion: Plan vs. Statement vs. Proposal

| Document | Length | Tone | What it's for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Statement** | 1–5 pages | Persuasive, narrative | Faculty job applications |
| **Plan** | 2–5 pages | Specific, projects, timelines | Faculty applications (EU); annual reviews; tenure case |
| **Proposal** | 5–15+ pages | Technical, methodology, budget | Funding agencies; sometimes faculty searches at research-heavy institutes |

**Critical:** if a brief asks for a "research proposal" rather than a "research statement", do not deliver a research statement. Treat as closer to a grant proposal — load `academic-grant-proposal` skill instead.

## Adjacent Documents Sometimes Conflated

These are NOT research statements; do not produce research-statement content for them:

- **Cover letter** — short (1 page); personalization, fit, document inventory.
- **Teaching statement** — pedagogy, courses, mentoring philosophy.
- **Diversity statement** (US) — DEI commitments, mentoring, community work.
- **Inclusive teaching statement** — pedagogy with DEI emphasis.
- **Statement of contributions** (Canada/Australia) — contributions to research, teaching, EDI; often combined.
- **Personal statement** (UK / postdoc) — broader narrative about career goals.

If the brief asks for any of these in addition to a research statement, **scope each separately**. Do not duplicate content across documents.

## Region-Phrase Common Pairings

| Region | Common document set |
|---|---|
| US R1 (CS/Eng) | Cover letter + Research Statement + Teaching Statement + Diversity Statement + CV + References |
| Dutch | Cover letter + Research Vision + Teaching Statement + CV (+ optional Leadership statement) |
| UK | Cover letter + Research Statement + Teaching Statement + CV (+ optional REF impact summary) |
| Canada | Cover letter + Research Statement + Teaching Dossier + EDI Statement + CV |
| Australia | Cover letter + Research Statement + Teaching Statement + EDI Statement + CV |
| Continental EU | Cover letter + Research Plan + Teaching Concept + CV + Habilitation file (some) |

## When the Brief Is Ambiguous

If the brief just says "research statement" without a length or further specification:

- Default to a **2-page statement** in the standard 5-section structure.
- Cover **vision + past + future + fit + closing** at the proportions in `length_tiers.md` Tier 2.
- Cap fit paragraph at ~10–15% — it is the most-region-variable section.

Always **flag the ambiguity** to the user and ask if they want a longer or shorter version, especially if the position is US R1 (where 3–4 pages is common).
