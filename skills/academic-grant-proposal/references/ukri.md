# UKRI / EPSRC / Royal Society Proposals

UK Research and Innovation umbrella covers seven research councils. CS/ML proposals primarily go to **EPSRC** (Engineering and Physical Sciences) with smaller roles for **MRC** (Medical), **STFC** (Particle/Astro/HPC), **AHRC** (Arts/Humanities, for digital humanities). Royal Society runs separate fellowship programmes.

## Mechanism Selection

### EPSRC

| Scheme | Eligibility | Budget | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard Research Grant** | Any UK academic | typically £100K-£2M | 1-5 years |
| **Open Fellowship** (replaces older fellowships) | Any career stage | salary + research | 3-5 years |
| **New Investigator Award (NIA)** | Within first 5 yr of academic role | up to £400K | up to 3 yr |
| **Programme Grant** | Established teams | £3-15M | 5-6 years |
| **Network Plus** | Community-building | £1-2M | 4-5 years |
| **Centres for Doctoral Training (CDT)** | Multi-PI consortium | ~£7M | 8 years |

EPSRC **prioritises** strategic themes (Trustworthy Autonomous Systems, AI for Science, Quantum, Sustainable Digital Society). Align with Themes when relevant.

### Royal Society

| Scheme | Eligibility | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **University Research Fellowship (URF)** | 3-8 yr post-PhD; outstanding early-career | 5+3 yr salary + research |
| **Dorothy Hodgkin Fellowship** | Career-disrupted early-career | 4 yr salary + research |
| **Royal Society Industry Fellowship** | Mid-career, academic↔industry secondment | 4 yr |
| **Wolfson Research Merit / Wolfson Fellowships** | Senior international recruits | 5 yr salary supplement |

URF is the UK's flagship early-career fellowship — comparable in selectivity to ERC StG.

### MRC, STFC, BBSRC, NERC, AHRC, ESRC

Same UKRI Funding Service portal. Each has its own remit and application templates; consult council-specific guidance before drafting.

## Funding Service: New Application Format

UKRI migrated to the **UKRI Funding Service** (launched 2022, rolling rollout). Replaced PDF Case for Support with **structured online forms** for many schemes. Key changes:

- **Word-counted online text boxes** instead of page-counted PDFs (typical: Vision 600 words, Approach 2000 words, Capability to Deliver 1000 words, Ethics 500 words — schemes vary)
- **Structured prompts**: each section asks specific questions; answer them directly
- **Multiple separate fields** instead of one Case for Support — apply the same logical structure but address each prompt fully
- **Static PDF preview** generated post-submission — proofread by exporting

**Always check the current scheme guidance** on the UKRI Funding Service before drafting — the prompts and word counts evolve.

## Case for Support (Standard Mode)

For schemes still using the legacy PDF Case for Support format (and as logical structure for the Funding Service prompts), typical structure:

### Part 1: Previous Track Record (~2 pp)
- PI's prior research arc and major outputs
- Co-Is' relevant track records
- Concrete metrics: papers, citations, software released, patents, prior grants delivered on-time

### Part 2: Description of Proposed Research and Its Context (~6-8 pp)

1. **Background and Vision** (~1.5 pp) — the field's current state, the gap, your hypothesis
2. **National Importance** — required at EPSRC: how the work supports UK economy, society, science. NOT optional.
3. **Aims and Objectives** (~0.5 pp) — numbered O1, O2, ...
4. **Research Plan / Workpackages** (~3-4 pp) — per WP: aim, methodology, milestones, deliverables, risks
5. **Programme of Work / Timeline** (~0.5 pp) — Gantt
6. **Management** (~0.5 pp) — for multi-investigator grants

### Part 3: Resources

Cross-references the separate Justification of Resources document (do not duplicate).

## Justification of Resources (J-aQR / JoR)

Standalone document (typically 2 pp) explaining and justifying every cost line in the budget. Required for most UKRI schemes.

Address per cost category:
- **Directly Incurred Staff** — PDRA salaries, technician time, PI buy-out. Justify why each role is needed and the FTE level.
- **Directly Allocated Staff** — PI/Co-I time at investigator rate (estimated, not invoiced). Justify time commitment matches the work plan.
- **Estates and Indirect Costs** — institutional rate, automatically calculated; brief acknowledgement.
- **Travel and Subsistence** — itemise conferences, fieldwork, collaboration visits with rationale.
- **Equipment** — items >£10K need quotes and justification of asset (not consumable).
- **Other Directly Incurred** — consumables, compute (cloud or HPC time), open-access publication fees.
- **Sub-contracts** — when work is procured from non-academic partners; must be justified vs. doing in-house.

UKRI uses **Full Economic Costing (fEC)**: institutions calculate total economic cost, UKRI pays 80% (council-dependent). Keep this distinction clear when budgeting.

## Pathways to Impact (legacy / Impact Statement)

UKRI removed standalone "Pathways to Impact" sections in 2020 — impact must now be **embedded throughout the application**. Address impact across:

- **Academic beneficiaries**: who in the field gains from the outputs (other UK groups, international, students)
- **Economic and societal beneficiaries**: industry partners, end-users, public services, policymakers
- **Engagement plan**: workshops, secondments, public engagement, open-source releases, datasets, standards
- **Industry engagement**: named partners with letters of support strengthen the case

For CS proposals, embed impact via:
- Open-source release plans with named maintainers
- CDT/PhD training contributions
- Industrial partnership commitments (matched effort, secondments)
- Policy engagement (e.g., Centre for Data Ethics, Alan Turing Institute, regulatory bodies)

## EPSRC Specifics

EPSRC's evaluation criteria (verbatim or near-verbatim across schemes):

1. **Quality**: novelty, importance, relationship to context, timeliness
2. **Importance**: contribution to research area, impact on other areas
3. **Applicant and Partnerships**: track record, expertise, collaborations
4. **Resources and Management**: justification, value for money

**National Importance** is a distinct EPSRC requirement: address how the work supports UK long-term economic competitiveness and societal challenges. Reference EPSRC's strategic delivery plans / themes.

EPSRC reviewers include both academic and industrial reviewers. Write for both audiences:
- Academic: rigour, novelty, evidence
- Industrial: impact, deployability, time-to-application

## Royal Society Fellowships

URF and similar fellowships use a separate Royal Society e-GAP portal (now also integrated with the Funding Service for some schemes). Distinct features:

- **5+3 year structure**: 5-year initial award + competitive 3-year extension based on mid-term review
- **Application centres on the PI**, not the project — Track Record section is heavily weighted
- **Project plan** (~5 pp) — usually shorter than EPSRC Case for Support
- **Independence**: applicants must demonstrate intellectual independence from PhD/postdoc supervisors. Co-authored papers with supervisors should be explained
- **Host Institution** must commit to a permanent post after fellowship end (HEI absorption commitment)
- **Two-stage selection**: shortlisting → interview at Royal Society HQ

Statement of motivation and career goals weigh substantially. Demonstrate: independence, ambition matching the scheme's prestige, and a 5-10 year research vision beyond the fellowship period.

## Auxiliary Documents

### Tailored CV / Résumé for Researchers and Innovators (R4RI)
UKRI piloted **résumé-style CVs** (R4RI) instead of conventional CVs across many schemes since 2021. Structure:

1. **Personal Details and Qualifications** — short
2. **Contributions to the generation of knowledge** — most relevant outputs (publications, patents, software, datasets) with narrative on contribution and impact
3. **Contributions to the development of individuals** — students supervised, mentees, training contributions
4. **Contributions to the wider research community** — peer review, editorial, conference organising, EDI activities
5. **Contributions to broader research and innovation users and audiences** — public engagement, industry, policy

R4RI rewards **diverse, narrative impact** over publication count. Adapt to the funder's specific R4RI template (council variants exist).

### Letters of Support
- Industry partners: matched effort, in-kind contributions, advisory roles
- Academic collaborators: data/sample/instrument access
- International collaborators: scope of partnership

### Data Management Plan (DMP)
UKRI requires a DMP for most schemes. Use the DCC checklist; address: types of data generated, metadata standards, sharing mechanism, repository, retention period, ethical/legal restrictions. Open-by-default expected unless justified otherwise.

## Submission Logistics

- **Portal:** UKRI Funding Service (https://funding-service.ukri.org/)
- **Royal Society:** flexi-grant or Funding Service depending on scheme
- **Institutional sign-off**: Research Office must approve before submission. Allow ≥2 weeks
- **Costing tool**: institution's TRAC/fEC tool generates budgets — supply work plan to research office early
- **Common rejection reasons:**
  - National Importance section missing or weak (EPSRC)
  - Track Record does not demonstrate independence (URF, NIA)
  - JoR does not justify each line, or contradicts the work plan
  - Impact embedded too thinly throughout — reviewers cannot find the engagement plan
  - Risk register absent or generic
  - For Funding Service: word-count overruns auto-truncate; sections answered partially
- **Resubmission:** UKRI generally permits one resubmission with substantive revision; uninvited identical resubmissions are discouraged

## CS / Systems / ML Notes for UK Funders

- **Alan Turing Institute** affiliations and EPSRC AI Hubs add weight when relevant
- **Trustworthy Autonomous Systems Hub**, **AI for Net Zero**, and **Responsible AI UK** are themed call structures — pick the matching call when available
- **DSIT** (Department for Science, Innovation and Technology) AI strategy alignment scores; reference the UK AI Safety Institute or AI Opportunities Action Plan when relevant
- Compute justification: **JADE2**, **Isambard-AI**, **Bristol AI Centre**, **Tier-1 HPC** (Archer2) access available — name in Facilities
- Impact case studies for CS proposals: open-source release with adoption metrics, datasets contributed, standards influenced
