# Regional Voice and Register

Voice mistakes are common because most published advice is calibrated to US R1 4-page statements. Other regions have materially different expectations.

## Comparison Matrix

| Axis | US R1 | UK | Dutch / Northern EU | Continental EU (DE/CH/Nordic) | Canada / Australia | Asia (Singapore / HK / Japan) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Length default | 3–4 pages | 1–2 pages | 2 pages | 2–3 pages | 2–3 pages | 2–3 pages |
| Future:past ratio | ~50:50 | ~60:40 future-heavy | ~60:40 future-heavy | ~50:50 | ~50:50 | ~50:50 |
| Personal voice | Discouraged | Light | **Required** ("from the heart") | Light–medium | Light | Light |
| Department fit | Light | Medium | **Heavy** | Medium-heavy | Medium | Medium |
| Funding narrative | Implicit | Medium | **Explicit** | **Explicit** | Medium-heavy | Medium |
| Hedging | Low | Medium | **Low** (Dutch directness) | Low–medium | Medium | Medium-high |
| Industrial collab | Optional | Optional | Counted ("valorization") | Counted | Counted | Counted |
| Self-praise tolerance | Medium | Low | **Very low** | Low | Low | Low |
| Title for "research statement" | Research Statement | Research Statement | Research Vision / Onderzoeksvisie | Research Plan / Forschungsplan | Statement of Research / Plan | Research Statement / Plan |

## US R1

- **Load first:** `us_school_guidance.md`. US university advice is internally varied; do not rely on one R1 template.
- **Length:** the job ad wins. General US career-center guidance often says 1–3 or 2–4 pages (CMU, Cornell, Penn, Yale, UConn, Notre Dame); STEM R1 / medical / lab fields often tolerate 3–5 pages (MIT, Harvard, Caltech); mathematics can run longer only with a 1-page accessible executive summary. Prepare a short and long version when unsure.
- **Voice:** persuasive, story-driven, but disciplined. Sells a vision. Reviewers expect "branding" — a clear research identity — but not hype.
- **Personal narrative:** discouraged in research statement (use diversity statement for personal narrative). Vision must come from the work, not autobiography.
- **Fit paragraph:** lighter than Dutch/EU, but still required when the statement must stand alone. Name facilities, centers, student populations, and collaborators only when they make future work feasible.
- **Funding:** explicit enough to show grant literacy. Name 1–3 realistic NSF / NIH / DOE / DARPA / foundation / industry vectors when appropriate; avoid a grant shopping list.
- **Institution type:** calibrate scope to R1, medical school, liberal-arts college, teaching-research university, or primarily undergraduate institution. The same future agenda cannot be pasted across these contexts unchanged.
- **Reviewer model:** overworked faculty from across the department, not just the subfield. They skim first. Paragraph 1, headings, and future-line titles carry disproportionate weight.
- **Top failure modes:** "bland and not sufficiently ambitious" (Philip Guo); future agenda doesn't flow from past; over-length; unexplained jargon/acronyms; no realistic funding/resource path; projects infeasible for the institution type.

## UK Russell Group

- **Length:** 1–2 pages typical. Cambridge/Oxford lectureships sometimes 3.
- **Voice:** restrained, scholarly, low-affect. British academic register.
- **REF alignment:** the brief sometimes asks how the candidate's work would map to the REF (Research Excellence Framework) panel. If so, name the panel.
- **Funding:** UKRI (EPSRC/BBSRC/ESRC/AHRC), Royal Society URF, Leverhulme. Naming 1–2 instruments by name is expected.
- **Fit paragraph:** medium weight. UK departments care about teaching-research integration; consider a single sentence on this.
- **Top failure modes:** US-style hype; over-length; missing REF alignment.

## Dutch / Northern EU

This is the most **distinctive** regional register and the most-needed adjustment for international applicants.

- **Length:** 2 pages is the safe default. 3 pages is the ceiling. UK-style 1-page risks looking thin.
- **Voice:** direct, evidence-linked, low-hedging. "Dutch directness" is a real cultural feature; phrasing that softens claims unnecessarily reads as insecure or evasive.
- **Document name:** often called "research vision" (onderzoeksvisie) rather than "research statement". The vision framing matters — it's more future-oriented, more strategic, more personal.
- **"From the heart" / intrinsic motivation:** explicitly requested in many briefs. **Not** an emotional disclosure. A personal scholarly credo. See `personal_voice.md`.
- **Fit paragraph:** **heaviest** of any region. "Inbedding" (embedding) is an explicit promotion criterion. Reviewers will read this paragraph carefully.
- **Funding:** **explicit and named.** NWO Veni → Vidi → Vici trajectory; ERC StG → CoG → AdG; Horizon Europe; Sectorplan. Reviewers want to see the candidate understands the funnel.
- **Industrial collaboration:** counts as "valorization" — a SEP (Strategy Evaluation Protocol) criterion. If you have industrial collaborations, name them.
- **Top failure modes:** generic praise instead of fit; over-hedging; missing funding narrative; US-style hype; emotional autobiography in place of intellectual credo.

### Dutch-specific phrasings to adopt

> "I see realistic funding pathways through NWO Veni in years 1–2 and ERC Starting Grant by year 4."

> "My research vision contributes to the department's strategic theme of [X]."

> "This work has natural valorization potential through [industrial partner / sector]."

### Dutch-specific phrasings to avoid

- "It would be a great honor to..." — too deferential.
- "I would be incredibly excited to..." — too emotional.
- "Your world-class department..." — sycophantic.
- Hedging stacks: "I think this might possibly be a way to potentially explore..."

## Continental EU (Germany / Switzerland / Nordic)

- **Length:** 2–3 pages. German "Forschungsplan" sometimes longer (3–5 pages).
- **Voice:** scholarly, structured, low-affect. Sometimes uses subheadings more than other regions.
- **Lab/chair structure:** German and Swiss positions often hire into a chair (Lehrstuhl) structure or a junior research group leader (Emmy Noether, ETH SNSF Eccellenza, EPFL tenure-track). Address how you would integrate.
- **Funding:** name DFG (Emmy Noether, Heisenberg, SFB), SNSF (Eccellenza, Sinergia), VR (Sweden), or relevant national instruments. ERC is universally expected.
- **Top failure modes:** ignoring Habilitation / chair-structure questions; missing DFG / SNSF naming; overly informal voice.

## Canada / Australia

- **Length:** 2–3 pages typical.
- **Funding:** NSERC Discovery + CRC (Canada Research Chair) for Canada; ARC DECRA / DP / FT for Australia. Naming is expected.
- **Indigenous research / decolonization:** some Canadian and Australian briefs explicitly ask candidates to address this. If asked, address concretely; don't perform.
- **Top failure modes:** copying a US statement without adjusting voice or funding language.

## Asia (Singapore / Hong Kong / Japan)

- **Length:** 2–3 pages typical, sometimes longer.
- **Voice:** scholarly, more deferential than European register but not subservient.
- **Funding:** name AcRF (Singapore), GRF (HK), KAKENHI (Japan), or relevant national instruments.
- **English variant:** institutions vary; default to American academic English unless the host institution clearly uses British.
- **Industrial / national alignment:** Singapore and HK reward alignment with national research priorities (often listed in strategic plans).

## Cross-Cutting Voice Rules

Regardless of region:

| Element | Choice |
|---|---|
| Person | First-person singular ("I") throughout |
| Tense | Past for completed work, present for ongoing identity, future for plans |
| Second-person | Avoid ("you can see..." — never) |
| "We" usage | Reserve for genuine collaborations, not as royal we |
| Sentence length | Mixed; short for declarative claims, longer for reasoning |
| Metaphor | Sparingly; only if it adds precision (Hamming "north star" works; "journey" doesn't) |
| Quotations | One maximum, only if it does load-bearing work |
| Jargon | Define on first use; assume field-literate but not subfield-expert reader |
| References | Cite own papers as footnotes or `[1]` if needed; avoid full bibliography unless asked |

## Translation Test

If you can take your statement and paste it into an application to a different region without major editing, you have not calibrated the voice. Specifically:

- A US-calibrated statement applied to a Dutch position will read as overconfident, generic in fit, and missing funding narrative.
- A Dutch-calibrated statement applied to a US position will read as understated, over-explicit on fit, and over-explicit on funding.
- A UK-calibrated statement applied to a US position will read as too brief and underspecified.

Re-edit per region. Do not single-source.
