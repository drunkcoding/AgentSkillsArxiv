## Mechanism Selection

| Mechanism | Eligibility | Budget | Duration | Page Limit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard / Small** | Any PI | $600K | 3 years | 15 pp Project Description |
| **Medium** | Any PI | $1.2M | 3-4 years | 15 pp |
| **Large** | Any PI | varies | up to 5 yr | 15 pp |
| **CAREER** | Untenured tenure-track within 7 yr of PhD; pre-tenure | $400K-$600K (CISE: $500K min) | 5 years (fixed) | 15 pp |
| **CRII** (CISE Research Initiation Initiative) | Untenured ≤3 yr from start of academic position | $175K | 2 years | 8 pp |
| **EAGER** | Any | $300K | 2 years | 8 pp |
| **RAPID** | Any (urgent) | $200K | 1 year | 5 pp |

**CISE-specific:** CISE Core programs (CCF/CNS/IIS) are **no-deadline** since 2018 — submit any time. Programs accept proposals on a rolling basis, reviewed in panels every 2-4 months.

## Proposal Components (PAPPG)

NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG, currently 24-1 / 25-1) defines required sections. **Read the current PAPPG before submitting.** Standard required documents:

1. **Cover Sheet** (auto-generated by Research.gov)
2. **Project Summary** — 1 page, three labeled sections
3. **Project Description** — up to 15 pages
4. **References Cited** — no page limit
5. **Biographical Sketch** (per senior personnel) — SciENcv format, 3-page limit
6. **Budget + Budget Justification** — itemized, 5-page narrative max
7. **Current and Pending (Other) Support** (per senior personnel) — SciENcv format
8. **Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources** — no page limit, narrative
9. **Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP)** — 2-page max (mandatory since 2025)
10. **Postdoc Mentoring Plan** — 1 page, only if budgeting postdocs
11. **Collaborators & Other Affiliations (COA)** — separate spreadsheet, conflict-of-interest disclosure

## Project Summary (1 page)

Three explicitly labeled sections (proposal **returned without review** if missing):

1. **Overview** — what is being proposed
2. **Intellectual Merit** — how the work advances knowledge
3. **Broader Impacts** — societal/educational/diversity outcomes

Each section starts with a literal bold heading, e.g. `**Intellectual Merit:**`. No figures, no citations, no equations. Reviewers see this first; ACs see only this when triaging.

## Project Description (15 pages)

No mandated subsections in PAPPG, but reviewers expect:

1. **Introduction / Motivation** (1-1.5 pp) — problem context, gap, what the project will deliver, contributions list
2. **Background and Related Work** (1-2 pp) — what is known, what is missing
3. **Research Plan / Thrusts / Aims** (8-10 pp) — typically 3-4 thrusts, each with: hypothesis, technical approach, preliminary results, evaluation plan, milestones
4. **Broader Impacts Plan** (1 pp) — distinct, not folded into thrusts
5. **Results from Prior NSF Support** (mandatory if PI/co-PI received NSF funding in prior 5 years) — list grant number, title, period, amount, summary of outcomes (≤5 pp total)
6. **Education / Outreach Plan** (only CAREER, otherwise folded into Broader Impacts)
7. **Timeline / Project Plan** (0.5 pp) — Gantt or year-by-year table
8. **Management Plan** (only multi-PI, ~0.5 pp)

CISE proposals: an **explicit "Broader Impacts" header** is expected as a top-level Project Description section, not just a paragraph.

## Intellectual Merit Criterion

NSF defines Intellectual Merit as the potential to advance knowledge. Reviewers score on:

- **Potential to advance knowledge.** Quantify: cite the SOTA result and what your project would beat or enable.
- **Creativity, originality, transformative concepts.** Differentiate from existing systems explicitly.
- **Plan is well-reasoned, well-organized, based on sound rationale.** Hypothesis-driven Aims, not method-driven.
- **PI qualifications.** 1-2 sentences in the relevant Aim ("PI led [system X], adopted by [users]") plus full evidence in Biosketch.
- **Adequate access to resources.** Compute, data, collaborators — referenced from the Facilities document.

## Broader Impacts Criterion

NSF defines Broader Impacts as "societal benefit or advancement of desired societal outcomes." This is **not boilerplate** and is graded equally with Intellectual Merit. Map to one or more of NSF's nine recognized categories:

1. Full participation of women, persons with disabilities, underrepresented minorities in STEM
2. Improved STEM education and educator development at all levels
3. Increased public scientific literacy
4. Improved well-being of individuals in society
5. Development of a diverse, globally competitive STEM workforce
6. Increased partnerships between academia, industry, and others
7. Improved national security
8. Increased economic competitiveness of the United States
9. Enhanced infrastructure for research and education

**CS/ML-specific Broader Impact strategies that score well:**

- Open-source release of artifacts (give GitHub URL of prior releases + stars/users)
- Curriculum integration: list a specific course at your institution that will adopt the work
- Undergraduate research (REU supplements): committed slots, recruitment from MSI/HBCU partners
- K-12 outreach via existing programs (e.g., university summer camps)
- Industry partnerships: name companies + concrete data/compute exchange
- Datasets/benchmarks released to community

**Avoid:** vague "we will give talks", "we will publish papers" — papers and talks are not Broader Impacts.

## CAREER-Specific Requirements

The Faculty Early Career Development (CAREER) Program funds early-career faculty who will lead **integrated research and education**. Page limit is still 15 pp Project Description, but content requirements differ:

- **Education plan must be integrated with research**, not a separate appendix — describe specific courses, undergraduate/graduate research integration, K-12 outreach
- **Departmental letter of support** required — signed by Department Head, confirms PI's protected research time and resources (not a recommendation letter; this is a structural requirement)
- **Career goal statement** in the project description — 5-year vision
- Single-PI only (no co-PIs); senior personnel and collaborators allowed
- Eligibility window is firm: **untenured, within 7 years of PhD** (verify exact rules in current solicitation)

CAREER reviewers explicitly evaluate the **integration** of research and education. A separate education paragraph reads as bolted-on; weave education through the Aims (e.g., "Aim 2 milestones become Lab 4 in [course]").

## CRII-Specific Requirements

CISE Research Initiation Initiative — for new CISE faculty in their **first 3 years**. Strict eligibility:

- PI must be in first 3 years of academic appointment (assistant professor or research assistant professor)
- PI cannot have been PI/co-PI on any prior federal research grant >$100K
- $175K total, 24 months, 8-page Project Description

CRII proposals are evaluated more on **promise** than preliminary results — reviewers expect novelty + plausibility, not extensive preliminary data. Frame as enabling a future larger NSF program.

## Auxiliary Documents

### Biosketch (NSF format, SciENcv-generated)
3 pages max, structured: (a) Professional Preparation, (b) Appointments and Positions, (c) Products (≤5 most relevant + ≤5 other significant), (d) Synergistic Activities. **Required to be generated via SciENcv** since 2024 — manual format is no longer accepted.

### Current & Pending (Other) Support
**SciENcv-generated PDF only** since October 2023. Lists all current and pending support (federal, foundation, foreign, in-kind) for each senior person. Failure to disclose foreign support is a major compliance issue.

### Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP)
2 pages max, mandatory. Per the 2025 update, DMSP must address: data types and volumes, formats, metadata standards, access/sharing mechanisms, archival plan, persistent identifiers, ethical/legal restrictions. CS proposals: code release plan (license, repository) belongs here.

### Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources
No page limit. Lists computational resources, lab equipment, datasets, collaborator access — anything that supports feasibility. CS/ML: list specific GPU clusters (institutional + ACCESS allocations + NAIRR), storage, datasets you have access to.

### Postdoc Mentoring Plan (if applicable)
1 page, required only if any postdoc is budgeted. Address: career counseling, training in lab management, training in grant writing, access to mentoring beyond PI, periodic evaluation.

## Submission Logistics

- **Portal:** Research.gov (Grants.gov is also accepted but Research.gov is preferred since 2023)
- **Compliance check:** institutional Sponsored Programs Office must approve before submission — start coordination ≥2 weeks before deadline
- **Common return-without-review (RWR) reasons:** missing Intellectual Merit / Broader Impacts headers in Summary, exceeding page limits, formatting violations (font <11pt, margins <1"), missing required documents
- **CISE no-deadline:** review takes 4-6 months from submission; plan accordingly
- **Solicitation deadlines:** verify in the specific program announcement — CAREER, NRT, NSF AI Institutes, etc. have fixed deadlines
